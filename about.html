Prior to pursuing a Computer Science Degree at Georgia Tech, I gained a curiosity for technology and programming from my initial exposure to online games as a child. Early online community games such as Millsberry, Runescape, and Fantage, to name a few, gaged my interest in learning on how programs, such as games, can bring communities together. And eventually, commencing innovations such as chat rooms and video calls demonstrated to me the potential that technology had.

Though, contradictory to what this background suggests, I grew up in school as an art student. Having spent a majority of my time in school learning and practicing art, I was able to gain fundamental visual arts skills that I now apply to my work. I treat the front-end of my work as my canvas, always making sure that it is ready for a gallery-style presentation. Due to my enjoyment of technology and the arts, this eventually led me to self-venture into graphic design, photoshop, and video and audio editing where I could practice both of my passions.

Realizing that my attachment to traditional art had dwindled, I decided to pursue a STEM field in high school and knew that computer science was my field of action because of its great calling for technological advancement and creativity. This decision led me to Georgia Tech where I have been attending since 2015.
